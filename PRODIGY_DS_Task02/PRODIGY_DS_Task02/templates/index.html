Patterns and trends discovered through exploratory analysis.

👩 Gender Was the Strongest Predictor

{{ stats.female_survival }}% female survival

Female passengers survived at a rate of {{ stats.female_survival }}% compared to just {{ stats.male_survival }}% for males — a reflection of the "women and children first" evacuation policy.

🎫 Class Determined Fate

{{ stats.class1_survival }}% 1st class

First-class passengers had a {{ stats.class1_survival }}% survival rate vs only {{ stats.class3_survival }}% for 3rd class. Proximity to lifeboats and social priority played a decisive role.

👶 Children Prioritised

Passengers under 12 years old (Children group) had significantly higher survival rates. Age, combined with gender, was a key factor in determining who reached the lifeboats.

👨‍👩‍👧 Optimal Family Size: 2–4

Solo travellers and very large families (7+) had lower survival rates. Those with 2–4 family members fared best — possibly helping each other reach lifeboats while not being too large a group to coordinate.

💰 Fare Correlates with Survival

Higher fare passengers (strongly correlated with 1st class) had better survival outcomes. The median 1st-class fare was several times higher than 3rd class, and so was their survival rate.

🚢 Embarkation Port Matters

Cherbourg (C) passengers had the highest survival rate, likely because a higher proportion of 1st-class passengers boarded there. Southampton (S) had the largest number but lower survival rate due to more 3rd-class passengers.
